# 1.3 Limitations of Existing System

The analysis of current freelance platforms reveals several systemic limitations that hinder the user experience for both clients and freelancers.

1. **High Platform Fees and Commissions:** Existing platforms charge freelancers service fees ranging from 5% to 20%, significantly reducing their net earnings. Combined with payment processing charges and currency conversion costs, the effective cost burden discourages talented professionals from using formal platforms.

2. **Review Manipulation and Fake Profiles:** The absence of rigorous verification mechanisms allows fake profiles with fabricated portfolios to exist on most platforms. Early positive reviews disproportionately influence ratings, incentivizing freelancers to accept low-paying projects solely to build a review history.

3. **Lack of Real-Time Communication Tools:** Most platforms rely on basic asynchronous messaging systems that lack real-time chat, file sharing, or collaboration features. This forces users to depend on external tools like WhatsApp or Zoom, fragmenting project communication and making dispute resolution difficult.

4. **Complex and Cluttered User Interfaces:** Years of feature accumulation without coherent redesign have left many established platforms with bloated interfaces. New users face steep learning curves, and the mobile experience on most platforms remains inferior to the desktop version.

5. **Delayed Payments and Financial Barriers:** Freelancer earnings are often held in escrow for extended periods, creating cash flow issues. High withdrawal fees and unfavorable currency exchange rates further reduce the actual income received by freelancers, particularly those in developing countries.

6. **Favoritism Toward Established Users:** Algorithm-driven job matching and search ranking on existing platforms consistently favor profiles with extensive histories. New but skilled freelancers struggle to gain visibility, creating a barrier to entry that limits the talent pool available to clients.
